5 Reasons To Not Skip Out On The French Quarters (NOLA)

1. Live Music.

I'm not going to give a semi-list of places that have live music because the majority of bars and cafe's in the French Quarters have live music, and I don't mean a DJ; I mean live aspiring singers and instumentalists with great vibes, tuning it out all day for those willing to listen. The talent and feel-good time is real here (I know it sounds cheesy).

These people are hardly getting outside of their comfort zone.

2. Museum/Galleries.

You want something quieter; more historical, then the museum is a great place to start. Not eveyone is looking for noise.

Don't miss out on the Cabildo and the Presbytere, to name a few. Also make stops at the low-key Art Galleries, like: Gallery Arlo or the Pamela Marquis Studio. If you want to try other galleries, try:

1. The Presbytère 2. The Cabildo 3. The New Orleans Jazz Museum at the Old U.S. Mint

3. Nightlife.

Most of the bars become somewhat of club scene. The entarnce is free. Bars are so close so can walk to most, if not all of them. Monday through Friday is more of a jazz-flow, more for the older soul. The weekend is where you see a lot of college kids hitting the strip. The music is more new school, hip-hop and techno.

4. Food.

Wow, there is a lot of food here. Don't miss out of New Orleans favorites like: fried oysters, crawfish, and PoBoy. If you're trying to go to more popular places, be ready to make a line. You're not the only with Google or Yelp, if you read up on it, everybody else has too. place like, .

5. Mardi Gras.

I saved this one for last not necessarily because it's the best (to many it is), but because it's not all year round and it very, very crowded. This may not be everybody's cup of tea, but to those that love the party scene, meeting ALOT of different people, this is the place. This time of year is where all the above (not so much the museum) blend together in the most high pumping way (so I've heard).
